What did Komodo dragons evolve from?

The evolutionary development of the Komodo dragon started with the genus Varanus, which originated in Asia about 40 million years ago and migrated to Australia, where it evolved into giant forms (the largest of all being the recently extinct Megalania), helped by the absence of competing placental carnivorans.

Are Komodo dragons descendants of dinosaurs?

komodoensis share a common ancestor with dinosaurs, but do not descend from them. Both V. komodoensis and dinosaurs belong to the subclass Diapsida which is branched into two infraclasses: Archosauria (dinosaurs, including birds) and Lepidosauria (tuataras, lizards, and snakes).

Is a Komodo Dragon a dragon or dinosaur?

The Komodo Dragon, tough considered by many intellectual groups to carry the same traits, characteristics and DNA strand that would otherwise link its ancestors to the Prehistoric Period, is by default only a very large reptile and not a dinosaur.

Are Komodo dragons ancient?

Komodo dragons are ancient

Though only discovered by Europeans in 1910, Komodos are considered a relict of giant lizards that have existed in Flores for at least 900,000 years. And fossils similar to Komodo dragons have been dated 3.8 million years old.

Is Komodo Prehistoric?

Fossils from Flores’ hot central region indicate that Komodo dragons first appeared on the island around one million years ago, and lived alongside pygmy elephants, giant tortoises and giant rats, and continued to do so for thousands of years.

Because dinosaurs became extinct around 65 million years ago the two creatures couldn’t have been around at the same time. That said, it’s very likely that dinosaurs are close ancestors of Komodo dragons. … Because of this, many experts believe that Komodo dragons are the closest living relative to dinosaurs today.

Are alligators and komodo dragons related?

No. Alligators, crocodiles, and komodo dragons are considered to be REPTILES — part of the phylogenetic group known as Diapsids. Komodo dragons are lizards, part of a group of reptiles known as Lepidosaurs. Alligators and crocodiles are part of a different group of reptiles, known as Archosaurs.

Are Komodo dragons only found in Indonesia?

Komodo dragons are only found in the wild on a handful of Indonesian islands where they are estimated to have roamed for millions of years. They draw visitors from around the world and in recent years authorities have grappled with how to manage conservation and tourism in the national park.
